Semester-wise Subjects
First Year Structure

S1 - Common Foundation

Common foundation semester for all branches. Exact science slot can vary by group and college timetable.

Engineering Mathematics I Engineering Physics / Chemistry Engineering Mechanics Basics of Civil & Mechanical Basics of Electrical & Electronics Programming in C / Computing Engineering Graphics Life Skills / Communication

Purpose: mathematics, science, engineering drawing, computing and basic engineering exposure.

S2 - Common Continuation

Common continuation semester with group-based science/electronics/electrical distribution.

Engineering Mathematics II Applied Physics / Chemistry Electronics / Electrical Systems Programming / Data Structures Basics Environmental Science Workshop / Engineering Practice Design Thinking / Ethics

Purpose: strengthen fundamentals before branch-specific subjects begin from S3.

Branch Groups

Group A - Computing & AI

CSE, IT, AI & Data Science, AI & ML, Cyber Security and related computing branches.

Group B - Electronics & Electrical

EEE, ECE, Applied Electronics & Instrumentation, Electronics & Computer, VLSI, embedded and communication branches.

Group C - Civil / Chemical / Bio

Civil, Civil & Environmental, Chemical, Food Technology, Biotechnology and related physical-science branches.

Group D - Mechanical & Production

Mechanical, Automobile, Mechatronics, Production, Industrial, Naval Architecture, Metallurgy and Materials branches.

S3-S8 Roadmap

S3-S4: Branch Entry

Core branch subjects begin. Expect stronger theory plus labs, assignments and microprojects.

S5-S6: Advanced Core

Specialized subjects, professional electives, labs and mini-project work become more important.

S7-S8: Final Year

Major project, seminar, internship or industry training, electives and placement preparation take priority.

S3 CSE Tips

Data Structures First

Practice arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees and graphs with small C/C++ or Java programs before exam season.

Build One Project Early

A small web app, campus tool, automation script or open-source contribution gives you something real for clubs, internships and interviews.

Use Git From Week One

Learn commits, branches, GitHub README files and issue tracking while your projects are still small.

Fresher Survival Notes
  • Save your staff advisor, CR, department office and hostel contact numbers.
  • Keep PDFs, lab records and assignment templates organized by semester.
  • Join one technical club and one non-technical activity if your schedule allows.
  • Ask seniors for PYQs, but study the module outcomes before memorizing answers.
  • Start placement basics gradually: aptitude, DSA, communication and resume projects.
